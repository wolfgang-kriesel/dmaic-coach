# Measurement system analysis (MSA)

Before trusting any data, confirm that the measurement system is adequate. If the
gauge is noisy, the "variation" you see may come from measurement, not the process.

## Gage R&R
Gage Repeatability and Reproducibility quantifies how much observed variation comes
from the measurement system. Repeatability is variation when the same operator
measures the same item repeatedly; reproducibility is variation between operators.

## Acceptance guidelines
A common guideline: a measurement system contributing less than 10% of total
variation (%R&R) is acceptable; 10–30% is marginal and may be tolerated depending
on the application; above 30% is unacceptable and must be improved first.

## Implication for the baseline
If MSA fails, fix the measurement system before baselining. A baseline built on an
inadequate gauge is not defensible, no matter how the numbers look.
