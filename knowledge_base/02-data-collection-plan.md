# Data collection plan

A data collection plan specifies what is measured, how, by whom, and under what
conditions, so that the data is representative and reproducible.

## Operational definitions
An operational definition removes ambiguity: it states exactly how a quantity is
measured so two people get the same number. For "handle time", define the start
event (call connected) and the stop event (wrap-up complete), the unit (seconds),
and the rounding rule.

## Sampling
Collect a sample that represents normal operating conditions: span multiple
operators, shifts, and days to capture real variation. Avoid convenience samples
taken from a single good day, which understate variation.

## Sample size
Larger samples give more stable estimates of mean and standard deviation. For a
baseline capability estimate, a sample of at least 30 individual measurements is a
common rule of thumb; more is better when variation is high.
