# Measure phase overview

The Measure phase turns a vague problem into a quantified baseline. Its purpose
is to establish, with trustworthy data, how the process actually performs today —
before any changes are made.

## Goals of Measure
- Define what to measure and how, with unambiguous operational definitions.
- Validate that the measurement system itself is trustworthy (MSA).
- Collect a representative sample of process output.
- Establish a baseline: process capability and a baseline sigma level.

## Why a baseline matters
Without a quantified baseline you cannot prove improvement later. The baseline is
the reference point against which Improve-phase changes are judged. A credible
baseline is grounded in validated data, not in opinion or anecdote.

## Common pitfalls
- Trusting a process owner's recollection of performance instead of data.
- Skipping measurement system analysis and baselining on noisy measurements.
- Reporting a mean while ignoring variation and the voice of the customer (specs).
